About me

UX Designer & Product Manager

With an academic background in neuroscience, cognitive science and philosophy, I'm a pragmatic product designer who sees the design process as a continuous problem-solving game.

My passion for design thinking derives from my academic exploration of human behavior. From programming sensorimotor robots to effectively navigate their environment, to analyzing the theoretical possibility of virtual reality, my studies culminated in a research thesis on the physiological and psychological processes underlying visual perception during dynamic scene viewing: Neurocinematics: An Empirical and Theoretical Investigation of Visual Attention and Event Segmentation during Dynamic Scene Viewing. And it is from this academic background that my ardor for design thinking sprang.

I see the world in terms of continuous goal-oriented actions and the design of everyday objects in relation to these actions. From idea to deliverable, I utilize human-interaction and design thinking principles to create simplistic, enjoyable and intuitive web and mobile experiences.

My Work

Project A

Objective
In preparation for a redesign, conduct an overhaul of a website and identify the site’s top priority UX issues.

Challenge
The website is unfamiliar, complex and outdated (i.e. no one has updated the site’s UI or navigation since its creation in 2007); additionally, the client is only vaguely familiar with their product and the original developer is no longer under their employment. What now?

My solution
While reviewing each page, I simultaneously created a site map. This allowed me streamline my review process, gain an in-depth understanding of the product and create the documentation I would later need to pinpoint usability issues to those unfamiliar with the site’s complexity.

Outcome
These site maps provided me with the ability to concretely identify the site’s top UX concerns: dead-end user flows, broken features, outdated content, and more. Using these site maps in conjunction with Google Analytics, I was able to lead constructive discussions with the client on how to simplify the site through the elimination of broken, unnecessary and underused aspects of the site.
